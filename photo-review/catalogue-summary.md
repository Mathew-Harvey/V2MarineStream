# Phase 3 — Catalogue Synthesis

Based on the 104 reviewed photographs/graphics (see photo-notes.md). Written 2026-07-15.

## 1. The story the photos tell

This is a library about **real underwater work, end to end**. Five strong narrative threads run through it:

1. **Inspection.** ROVs with lights glowing beside monumentally fouled mooring chains in open blue water (the GOPR series), plus pure open-water ROV frames (ROV -26, ROV -27). This is the most visually striking thread and the most on-brand.
2. **Cleaning.** Yellow and lime hull-crawlers magnetically attached to ship sides at the waterline, night operations, and authentic HUD screen captures showing fouled-versus-cleaned hull with live telemetry (SAIPEM and F12056/TK8 series). Raw but credible, the proof layer.
3. **Capture and filtration.** The Franmarine containerised treatment plant, both documentary (bright quayside, hoses out, discharge plume) and a purpose-graded cinematic hero set (HERO_BANNER/HERO_FULL files). The environmental-compliance differentiator.
4. **Scale and clients.** Drone aerials of offshore support vessels mid-mooring-operation (Pacific Centurion set, DJI_0248 stern with buoy and crew), a drillship, dry docks, and a submarine alongside in turquoise water (Collins Class hint). Defence and offshore energy credibility.
5. **People and knowledge.** Candid crew portraits with hardware, control-room and dive-supervision interiors, and a large set of training/demonstration events (MTCC Africa and shipyard demos). Human, slightly rough, best used small.

Add a sixth emotional register: **golden-hour harbour calm** (image000005/7/8/9, DJI_0221/0222), dawn departures and dusk returns. A deliberate warm counterpoint to an otherwise cool catalogue.

## 3. Palette synthesis

The catalogue is decisively **cool**: deep ocean blues and cyans underwater, steel greys on deck, charcoal graded equipment. The recurring accents are **safety orange and buoy yellow**, which conveniently harmonise with the existing brand orange `#ff6600`.

## 4. Gaps and weak spots

- **No hero-grade diver imagery.** Commercial diving is claimed capability, but the only diver shots are thumbnail-grade (Picture2, FS0093). Worth a future shoot.
- **Everything caps at ~2000 px** from the compression pass. Fine to 1920-wide heroes, but request originals of the top ten, especially Screenshot 2025-06-18 (crawler on a bow above the harbour skyline, the most arresting content in the set, stored at 545 px).
- **No clean before/after cleaned-hull pair** free of HUD overlays; the story exists only inside screen captures.
- **People coverage is candid phone-grade.** Fine for authenticity tiles, but no polished team portrait set.
- **Only 8 portrait-orientation photos**, which limits mobile hero crops; the strong blues (ROV -27, GOPR set) crop vertically well, which mitigates this.
- **No platform/software UI assets** in this drop beyond burned-in HUD frames; the current site's dashboard PNGs are low grade.
