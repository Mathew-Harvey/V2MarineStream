## Response to Sam (DPIRD WA)

---

**To:** Sam, Careena, Claire  
**Subject:** RE: Hull Biofouling ID Guide — Updated Based on Your Feedback (v3.0)

---

Hi Sam, Careena, and Claire,

Thank you so much for taking the time to review the Hull Biofouling ID Guide and providing such thorough, constructive feedback. Your input has been invaluable in making this resource accurate, compliant, and genuinely useful for WA operations.

I've implemented all your recommendations. Here's a detailed summary of the changes in **Version 3.0**:

### 1. Species Selection — Aligned with WA Noxious Fish List (Schedule 5)

Following your advice, I've removed species that are **not on the WA noxious list** and are not of concern to DPIRD:

| Removed Species | Reason |
|-----------------|--------|
| *Didemnum perlucidum* (White Colonial Sea Squirt) | Only reportable in Montebello Islands; likely to be delisted |
| *Styela clava* (Stalked Sea Squirt) | Not on WA noxious list |
| *Styela plicata* (Pleated Sea Squirt) | Not on WA noxious list |
| *Ciona intestinalis* (Vase Tunicate) | Not on WA noxious list |

The guide now clearly states it features **key species from the WA noxious list** and prominently includes a link to the full Schedule 5 list at fish.wa.gov.au.

### 2. Priority System — Simplified & Aligned with DPIRD Protocol

I've replaced the confusing HIGH/MEDIUM priority system with clearer terminology:

| Old System | New System | Meaning |
|------------|------------|---------|
| HIGH | **NOXIOUS** | WA noxious listed species — Stop work, report to DPIRD within 24 hours |
| MEDIUM | **WATCH** | Emerging threats not yet established in WA — Document & report |
| N/A | **NATIVE** | Normal WA fouling, no report needed |

The reporting protocol now explicitly instructs divers to:
- **Report to DPIRD within 24 hours**
- **Cease work until DPIRD advises** how to proceed in a biosecure manner
- **Leave specimens intact** — do not remove suspected noxious organisms

### 3. Corrected Establishment Status

| Species | Previous Status | Corrected Status |
|---------|-----------------|------------------|
| European Shore Crab (*Carcinus maenas*) | Implied established | **NOT established in WA** — remains noxious listed, must be reported |
| Wakame (*Undaria pinnatifida*) | "Already in southern Australia" | **Established in VIC/TAS/SA but NOT yet detected in WA** |

### 4. Oyster Identification — Generalised per Your Recommendation

Changed from species-specific "Pacific Oyster" to general **"Large Non-Native Oysters (Magallana spp.)"** acknowledging:
- Species ID is virtually impossible morphologically in-situ
- Includes *M. gigas* (Pacific), *M. bilineata* (Black Scar), and *M. ariakensis* (Suminoe)
- Divers are now instructed to photograph any large oysters for lab identification

### 5. Updated Contact Information

All materials now show the correct WA reporting contacts:

| Contact Method | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| **FishWatch Hotline** | 1800 815 507 |
| **Email** | Aquatic.Biosecurity@dpird.wa.gov.au |
| **Mobile App** | MyPestGuide Reporter |

I've removed the outdated national marine pest reporting website reference as requested.

### 6. Added Full Noxious List Reference

Each page now includes a clear disclaimer:
> *"This guide features key species from the WA noxious fish list. View the full list at fish.wa.gov.au or Schedule 5, Fish Resources Management Regulations 1995."*

### 7. Additional Species Added

- **Northern Pacific Seastar** (*Asterias amurensis*) — Added as WA noxious listed species
- Updated location notes to include **Henderson alongside HMAS Stirling** for CSS detections

### 8. WA Reporting Notes Section

Clarified the purpose of this section and updated with:
- HMAS Stirling & Henderson CSS detection notes
- European Shore Crab status in WA
- Wakame status in WA
- Clear instruction to view full noxious list

### 9. Image Credits

Added proper attribution footer: "Images: CSIRO, DPIRD WA, Wikipedia Commons, DAFF"

### 10. Materials Updated

- ✅ Main web application (index.html)
- ✅ WA A5 Pocket Booklet
- ✅ WA A3 Wall Poster

### Outstanding Items for Future Updates

1. **Navy Reporting Protocols** — Will consult with NavySafE about chain of command notifications (per separate feedback)
2. **Other Jurisdiction Guides** — Will review NSW, QLD, VIC, TAS, SA, NT guides against each state's requirements
3. **DPIRD Pest Alerts** — Have reviewed your published fact sheets and aligned our information accordingly

Thank you again for your guidance in getting this right. Your expertise ensures this resource will genuinely help protect WA waters from marine pests.

Happy to discuss any of these changes further or make additional adjustments.

- Updated the oyster section to be more general since species ID is difficult in-situ

**3. Immediate Use**  
The posters and pocket guides are ready for printing! The WA versions have just been updated to v3.0 with all the feedback incorporated.

### Navy Reporting Protocols — Request for Consultation

You raised a really important point about Navy's chain of command for reporting detections on Navy vessels. I completely agree that:
- NavySafE should be notified first before regulator notification
- The notification to regulators should be made by Navy

I'd love to get this right. **Would you be able to facilitate an introduction to NavySafE** so I can discuss:
- The appropriate contact details to include
- The preferred wording for the Navy reporting pathway

Once I have their input, I'll create a dedicated "Navy Vessels" section that makes it crystal clear:
1. Navy chain of command first (NavySafE contacts)
2. Navy notifies regulator
3. Diver supports with photos/documentation

### What's in the Current Version (v3.0)

The guide now includes:
- **NOXIOUS** priority species (per WA noxious fish list)
- **WATCH** species (emerging threats not yet established)
- **NATIVE** species for comparison
- Clear reporting instructions (FishWatch 1800 815 507, DPIRD email, MyPestGuide app)
- Niche area inspection guidance (sea chests, rope guards, etc.)

### Normal Biofouling Information

You mentioned feedback about wanting more info on "normal stuff" — I've kept the Native species section which covers common WA fouling (blue mussels, native tube worms, native sea squirts, barnacles, bryozoans, etc.). This helps teams:
- Distinguish invasive from native species
- Complete accurate PMP Hull Inspection reports
- Know what doesn't need reporting

Let me know if you'd like me to expand this section further with more native species for the Navy guides specifically.

### Next Steps
