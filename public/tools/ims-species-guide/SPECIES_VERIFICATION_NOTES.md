### Official Priority Species (Hull Fouling Relevant):
1. **Carpet sea squirt** (Didemnum vexillum) - DETECTED at HMAS Stirling & Henderson
2. **Asian green mussel** (Perna viridis)
3. **Black-striped mussel** (Mytilopsis sallei)
4. **Asian paddle crab** (Charybdis japonica)
5. **European green crab** (Carcinus maenas) - NOT established in WA
6. **Northern Pacific seastar** (Asterias amurensis)
7. **Wakame/Japanese kelp** (Undaria pinnatifida) - NOT in WA
8. **Zebra mussel** (Dreissena polymorpha) - freshwater but hull-carried

### Current App Status: ✅ CORRECT - matches DPIRD website

## NSW - DPIRD (https://www.dpi.nsw.gov.au/fishing/aquatic-biosecurity/pests-diseases/marine-pests)

### Official Priority Species:
**Bivalve Molluscs:**
- Asian clam
- Asian date/bag mussel (Arcuatula senhousia)
- Asian green mussel (Perna viridis)
- Black-striped mussel (Mytilopsis sallei)
- Pacific Oyster (established - movement controls)
- Soft shell clam

**Crustaceans:**
- Asian paddle crab (Charybdis japonica)
- Asian shore crab (Hemigrapsus sanguineus)
- Brush-clawed shore crab
- Chinese mitten crab (Eriocheir sinensis)
- European green crab (established in southern NSW)

**Seaweed:**
- Caulerpa taxifolia (ESTABLISHED - 14+ estuaries - Class 1 Noxious)
- Japanese seaweed/Wakame (NOT in NSW)

**Other:**
- Colonial sea squirt (includes Carpet sea squirt)
- European Fan Worm (established - Eden, Botany Bay, Sydney)
- Northern Pacific Seastar (prohibited matter)
- Non-native sea squirts

### Established in NSW:
- Caulerpa taxifolia ⚠️
- European Fan Worm (Eden since 1996)
- European Shore Crab (20+ southern estuaries)
- Pacific Oyster

### Current App Status: ✅ CORRECT
- Caulerpa taxifolia shown as "CLASS 1" NOXIOUS with note about 14+ estuaries ✅
- European Fan Worm correctly marked as "EST" (Established) with Eden 1996 note ✅
