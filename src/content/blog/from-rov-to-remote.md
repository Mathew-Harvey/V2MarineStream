> *Nine months ago, I stepped into the marine industry with little practical experience but massive willingness to learn. From filtration systems to ROV operations, here's how MarineStream transformed my understanding of what's possible in marine technology.*

Growing up in Perth, I was always drawn to the ocean. Whether it was through boating, exploring the coastline, or observing marine life, that natural curiosity slowly evolved into a deep passion for marine systems and sustainability. I even took a brief detour into Marine Biology studies, which only intensified my interest in how we interact with and preserve our ocean environments. However, it wasn't until I joined Franmarine and MarineStream that this passion really found its purpose.

Just over nine months ago, I stepped into the marine industry with little to no practical experience but with a massive willingness to learn. I had recently completed a Defence Industry Pathways Program traineeship, which gave me my first proper insight into the sector. That experience was a game-changer. It offered me a glimpse into what my future in the marine world could look like and made me determined to take every opportunity that came my way.

![MarineStream training session](/images/blog/from-rov-to-remote/3.JPEG)
*Learning the fundamentals of MarineStream during my early training days*

At Franmarine, I was fortunate to begin working alongside industry professionals, from commercial divers and project managers to technology specialists and engineers. My initial focus was on our In-Water Hull Cleaning (IWHC) filtration units, which quickly became my area of expertise. I found myself becoming the go-to person for all things filtration-related, and this hands-on experience gave me a real sense of ownership and purpose.

As I started branching out into our broader technology systems, I found myself becoming increasingly fascinated by two areas: ROV (Remotely Operated Vehicle) operations and the asset management software we power; MarineStream. The combination of both technologies opened my eyes to just how transformative digital systems can be in the marine environment. Not only are they improving operational efficiency, but they're also paving the way for more sustainable practices in ocean monitoring and maintenance.

## Taking Control: My First ROV Experience

When the opportunity to learn how to operate ROVs came up, I jumped at it, though I'll admit, I was a bit apprehensive at first. I didn't know what to expect. Operating underwater robots sounded technical, and I wasn't sure if I'd pick it up quickly. But thanks to a training session led by our ROV Manager, who has an extensive background in the field, my fears were quickly put to rest.

The session walked me through everything: from setup and pack-down to the actual control interface. To my surprise, the controls were more intuitive than I expected. Using a simple hand controller, I was navigating these machines underwater in no time. There was something oddly satisfying about piloting a robot beneath the waves and seeing the marine world from its perspective.

![ROV operations team](/images/blog/from-rov-to-remote/1.jpg)
*Working alongside the experienced ROV operations team during my first hands-on training*

Since that first session, I've been involved in multiple ROV jobs, ranging from mooring inspections to hull condition assessments and biofouling surveys. I've also worked with our tech team to help maintain these ROVs, which is crucial because they often endure incredibly harsh underwater conditions. It's given me a real appreciation for both the capability and the fragility of marine tech.

## Diving Into MarineStream

Around the same time, I was introduced to MarineStream, our all-in-one asset management and reporting software. As someone who picks up new technology quickly, I was genuinely excited to explore its capabilities. Even though it's a powerful platform with a lot of moving parts, I found myself navigating it with ease. The more I used it, the more I appreciated how intuitive and well-designed it really is. It didn't take long before I was confidently using MarineStream to manage assets, log operations, and streamline reporting across multiple projects.

MarineStream allows us to manage assets and record operations in real time. You can capture both quantitative data (like temperature, depths, or positioning) and qualitative observations (like biofouling levels or hull damage) all within the same interface. One of the most impressive features is the ability to communicate with stakeholders and other team members directly within the job dashboard, live, as the operation unfolds.

![MarineStream inspection interface](/images/blog/from-rov-to-remote/2.jpg)
*The MarineStream platform in action during a hull inspection, showing real-time data capture and collaboration features*

But perhaps the most revolutionary aspect is its integration with ROV operations. You can remotely control or monitor an ROV and capture all footage, notes, and insights directly into the MarineStream platform, even if you're nowhere near the site.

## My First Remote MarineStream Job

That leads me to one of the most exciting experiences I've had so far: my first remote MarineStream job.

I was sitting in my home office in Perth, coffee in hand, when I received an email invite to join a live job happening in Sydney. The task was a biofouling inspection under the hull of a large vessel, and the ROV operator on-site was someone from our NSW team. With one click, I was dialled in and watching the operation unfold in real time through MarineStream.

![Remote collaboration workspace](/images/blog/from-rov-to-remote/ab412fb3-8579-42fa-a4f9-8b50bfb36774.jpg)
*My home office setup during the remote MarineStream job - connecting Perth to Sydney in real-time*

What blew me away was the interactivity. As the ROV operator navigated different sections of the vessel, I followed along using the digital twin inside the MarineStream platform. I was capturing live screenshots of what the ROV was seeing, taking condition notes, and even chatting with the operator in real time about what we were observing. It genuinely felt like I was on-site, despite being thousands of kilometres away.

Once the job wrapped up, after just under three hours, we collaboratively reviewed the data, selected the best images for the report, and finalised our notes. A comprehensive inspection report was generated on the spot, complete with timestamps, annotated photos, and contextual commentary from team members on opposite sides of the country.

![Marine inspection results](/images/blog/from-rov-to-remote/Image.jpg)
*Real-time hull inspection data captured during the remote collaboration session*

I stepped away from my desk in awe. The entire experience felt futuristic. The fact that I could contribute meaningfully to a marine inspection from a completely different state not only shows how powerful MarineStream is but also what the future of this industry might look like.

## Looking Forward

Reflecting on the past few months, I'm proud of how far I've come. From being a trainee with minimal industry knowledge to now driving ROVs, running high-tech filtration systems, and collaborating on remote inspections. It's been a wild ride. More importantly, it's made me optimistic about the future of the marine industry.

With tools like MarineStream and a growing focus on tech-driven sustainability, I genuinely believe we're heading toward a smarter, cleaner, and more collaborative marine environment. And I'm excited to be part of that journey both above and below the waterline.

> **The future of marine operations isn't just about better technology—it's about breaking down geographical barriers and enabling expertise to flow wherever it's needed most.**
